History and Surroundings

Protaras is a tourist resort which comes under the administrative jurisdiction of Paralimni Municipality in Cyprus. In ancient times stood the old city-state of Leukolla here. The city used to be a small safe harbour in the 4th century BC. Protaras is also referred to as "the land of windmills", maintaining the nostalgic quality of the past. The town of Protaras is located northeast of the resort of Ayia Napa, right on the east coast of the island of Cyprus. Here, Aphrodite's island boasts a landscape of sandy beaches, windmills and fig trees. The Valley of the Windmills is especially magical in the late afternoon, when the white windmills contrast with the dark blue evening sky. Protaras is known as a diving area. The most popular diving location is Green Bay with underwater sculptures for thousands of beginners to enjoy while testing their diving skills.

The capital of Cyprus is Nicosia. It became the real commercial, political and cultural center of the country. The Old Town, the Cyprus Museum, the Famagusta Gate and the Archbishop's Palace are worth a visit in the metropolis. Abundant shops invite you to buy gold, pottery and other traditional products. Nicosia is located in the interior of Cyprus, about 20 km from the northern coast and 40 km from the southern coast as the crow flies. The city is dependent on the international airport of Larnaca, which is connected to it by the A1/A2 highway. Population of Nicosia Nicosia (over 300,000 inhabitants in total, including agglomerations, of which 4 fifths live in the southern part) is the capital of Cyprus, divided into two parts by the so-called green line since 1974. The northern part is under the administration of the Turkish Cypriots, the southern part is under the administration of the Republic of Cyprus with a preponderance of Greek residents. The old town of Nicosia is home to countless monuments, including the Venetian walls, which date back to the time when the Venetians ruled Cyprus.
